## **5 FAQs: URL Shorteners for Twitter**

### **Q1. Does Twitter automatically shorten links?**

Yes, Twitter wraps every link using its own t.co system. But t.co gives you no branded domain, no custom alias, and no detailed analytics beyond basic impressions. A third-party shortener fills that gap.

### **Q2. Do short links hurt SEO on Twitter?**

No. Short links on Twitter do not hurt SEO because Twitter links are `nofollow` by default, meaning search engines do not follow them for ranking signals. The benefit is in user trust and click tracking, not SEO juice.

### **Q3. Can I use a branded domain for Twitter short links?**

Yes. Tools like SimpleURL, Rebrandly, and Bitly all support custom branded domains. Read our [**complete branded domain setup guide**](https://blog.simpleurl.tech/how-to-build-trust-with-custom-branded-domains-complete-setup-guide) to get started step by step.

### **Q4. What is the best free URL shortener for Twitter?**

SimpleURL offers a free 15-day trial with full features. Check our full list of [**free URL shorteners with analytics**](https://blog.simpleurl.tech/free-url-shortener-with-analytics) to compare your options before choosing.

### **Q5. How do I track which tweet drove the most clicks?**

Use [**UTM parameters**](https://blog.simpleurl.tech/utm-parameters-beginners-guide-2026) on every link you post. Each tweet gets a unique UTM tag (for example, `utm_content=tweet1` vs `utm_content=tweet2`). Then check your dashboard or Google Analytics to see which tweet drove more traffic.
