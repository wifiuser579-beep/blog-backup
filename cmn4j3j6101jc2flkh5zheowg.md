## **The TRAI DLT Compliance Angle Nobody Talks About**

![](https://cdn.hashnode.com/uploads/covers/682870ca34178af533acf2f9/3f28c633-c06f-42b8-9274-e286c99c42ed.png align="center")

Short links directly help Indian businesses stay SMS-compliant under TRAI rules.

### **What Is TRAI DLT?**

TRAI's DLT system regulates all commercial SMS messages sent in India. Every SMS template must be pre-registered. Templates are character-limited and must match an approved format exactly.

### **How Long URLs Create Compliance Problems**

*   A long URL inside a registered template changes the character count
    
*   If your live SMS does not match the registered template structure, it gets flagged or blocked
    
*   Long URLs often push messages beyond the 160-character single SMS limit, splitting the message and breaking the template match
    

### **How Short Links Solve This**

*   A short link occupies 20 to 25 characters instead of 80 to 120
    
*   It keeps your SMS within the registered template structure
    
*   It ensures your message stays as one SMS unit, both for compliance and cost
    

> **Pro Tip: Always use a consistent short link domain in your DLT-registered SMS templates. This makes future campaign swaps easy without needing to re-register a new template.**

## **Best Practices for Short Links in Messaging Campaigns**

*   **Use a branded or custom alias** for every campaign-facing link. Random alphanumeric slugs look suspicious.
    
*   **Test every link before sending.** Click it yourself on both Android and iOS before the broadcast goes out.
    
*   **Match your UTM tags to your campaign names.** Consistent naming in [simpleurl.tech/utm-builder](https://www.simpleurl.tech/utm-builder) makes Analytics reporting much cleaner.
    
*   **Do not hide the destination.** Customers should be able to guess where the link leads from the alias itself. `smurl.tech/free-trial` is better than `smurl.tech/x7k2p`.
    
*   **Set link expiry for time-sensitive offers.** A Diwali sale link that stays active in January creates confusion and damages trust.
